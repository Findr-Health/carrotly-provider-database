### 1.3 How PBMs Make Money

Understanding PBM revenue is critical to identifying partnership value:

**Revenue Stream 1: Administrative Fees**
- Charged to health plans/employers
- Typically $3-$8 per member per month
- MedImpact: ~$150-$400M/year (50M members x $3-8 PMPM)

**Revenue Stream 2: Rebates**
- Drug manufacturers pay to secure formulary placement
- Can be 20-50% of drug's list price
- **Traditional PBM Model:** Keep most rebates, pass through small %
- **MedImpact Model:** Claims to pass through more (not verified)

**Revenue Stream 3: Spread Pricing**
- PBM charges plan sponsor $100 for drug
- PBM pays pharmacy $80
- PBM keeps $20 "spread"
- **Increasingly scrutinized** by regulators

**Revenue Stream 4: Mail-Order Pharmacy**
- MedImpact operates "Birdi" mail pharmacy
- 90-day supply orders (higher margin)
- Convenience for members, revenue for PBM

**Revenue Stream 5: Specialty Pharmacy**
- "MedImpact Direct Specialty"
- High-cost drugs (cancer, MS, etc.)
- Higher margins due to complexity

**Total Annual Revenue (Estimated):**
- ~$1.5-2.5 billion (private company, not disclosed)
- Compared to: CVS Caremark ~$170B, Express Scripts ~$120B, Optum Rx ~$110B

## PART 4: CONSUMER BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS

As a behaviorist, I must address: **Will members actually use Clarity AI?**

### 4.1 Behavioral Psychology Principles

**Principle 1: Loss Aversion**
> People feel the pain of losing $20 twice as strongly as the pleasure of gaining $20.

**Application:**
When Clarity AI says "You'll save $20 at Walmart vs. CVS," members feel they're **losing** $20 by going to CVS. This drives behavior change.

**Evidence:**
- Kahneman & Tversky, Prospect Theory (1979)
- Loss aversion motivates 2x more than equivalent gain

---

**Principle 2: Choice Architecture (Nudging)**
> The way choices are presented dramatically affects decisions.

**Application:**
Clarity AI uses visual hierarchy:
- ðŸ’š Green = Preferred (saves money)
- ðŸŸ¨ Yellow = Standard (costs more)
- Clear dollar amounts ($10 vs $30)
- Distance displayed (convenience factor)

**Evidence:**
- Thaler & Sunstein, "Nudge" (2008)
- 40-60% behavior change with proper choice architecture

---

**Principle 3: Friction Reduction**
> Every additional step reduces completion rate by 20-30%.

**Application:**
Current member experience:
```
1. Get prescription
2. Call pharmacy
3. Give them Rx info
4. Wait on hold
5. Drive to pharmacy
6. Wait 20 minutes
7. Discover copay is $60
8. Decide whether to fill
Total: 8 steps, 60+ minutes
```

Clarity AI experience:
```
1. Ask "Where should I fill this?"
2. Click "Transfer to Walmart"
3. Drive to Walmart
4. Pick up (copay already known: $10)
Total: 4 steps, 20 minutes
```

**Result:** 50% faster, 50% fewer steps = Higher completion rate

---

**Principle 4: Social Proof**
> People follow others' behavior, especially in uncertain situations.

**Application:**
Clarity AI shows:
- "82% of members in your area use Walmart Pharmacy"
- "4.5/5 stars from 12,483 reviews"
- "Most popular choice for diabetes medications"

**Evidence:**
- Cialdini, "Influence" (1984)
